## Overview

ShowStack's Signal Flow Diagrammer is now a complete end-to-end product. Engineers can author signal-flow diagrams on a live JointJS canvas with smart shapes linked to ShowStack equipment records (Console, Device, SpeakerArray, CommBeltPack, Generic), typed orthogonal connectors with five signal types, the full canvas UX (pan/zoom/snap/undo/multi-select/keyboard delete), and now — as of v2.2 — debounced autosave with optimistic-locked conflict handling, label propagation, and ghost rendering of orphaned equipment references.

The defining v2.2 outcome: **the Save button is gone**. Edits persist automatically within 1.5 seconds; multi-tab conflicts reveal a locked-copy banner with a hard reload path; tab-close edits flush via keepalive; equipment renames propagate on next diagram load; deleted equipment renders ghosted so the engineer can re-link or remove.

## Milestone Summary

**Decimal Phases:** None — milestone shipped without insertions.

**Key Decisions:**

- **Custom event-sourced undo stack over JointJS+ CommandManager** (Phase 8 research). `@joint/core` 4.2.4 (MPL-2.0) is the open-source release; CommandManager + Clipboard are JointJS+ commercial only. ~120 LOC of project-owned undo code beats a commercial dependency.
- **Optimistic-lock atomic UPDATE over `select_for_update()`** (Phase 9). Single DB round-trip; `rowcount == 0` is the conflict signal. No row-level locks held across the autosave POST.
- **No `beforeunload`, no `sendBeacon` for keepalive** (Phase 9). `beforeunload` cancels the fetch; `sendBeacon` has a 64 KB payload cap that's smaller than typical diagrams. `keepalive: true` on `fetch()` with `visibilitychange`/`pagehide` is the documented path.
- **`If-Match` header NOT enforced on viewport-only saves** (Phase 9, D-05). Viewport (pan/zoom/snap) stays last-write-wins by design — engineers shouldn't get a 409 banner because another tab changed zoom level.
- **Server enrichment on GET, not on persist** (Phase 9). `canvas_state` blob is never mutated on the wire — `_enrich_nodes()` deep-copies before writing `data.label` + `data.isOrphan`, so the persisted blob remains immutable and labels stay live across equipment renames.
